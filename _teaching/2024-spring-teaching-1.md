**Objectives:**

To master statistical methods for data collection, organization, and interpretation to support evidence-based decision-making in biology and public health.

**Key Topics**
Descriptive Statistics: Variable types, frequency tables, measures of central tendency (mean, median), and dispersion (variance, standard deviation).

Probability Theory: Binomial, Poisson, and Normal distributions applied to health data.Sampling: Random sampling techniques, confidence intervals, and sample size determination.

Inferential Statistics: Hypothesis testing ($H_0$ vs. $H_a$), Type I and II errors, and Z/t-tests for mean comparisons.

**Methodology**

Combines theoretical seminars with practical workshops, emphasizing data analysis through computational tools for clinical and biological datasets.
