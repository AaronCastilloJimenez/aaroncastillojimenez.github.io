--- title: "Automated 3D Cell Segmentation for Root Meristem Analysis" excerpt: "A deep learning-based pipeline for segmenting Arabidopsis root cells using three fluorescent reporters and CNN architectures (PlantSeg & Cellpose)." collection: portfolio --- This project, currently under development, provides a specialized workflow for the automated analysis of confocal microscopy stacks in `.nd2` format. The goal is to characterize the cellular geometry and cell cycle dynamics of the *Arabidopsis thaliana* apical meristem. ### Key Features: * **Multi-Channel Analysis:** Processing of three distinct fluorescent markers: * **Cell Wall Marker:** Used for defining cell boundaries and geometry. * **Cell Cycle Reporters:** Two markers used to visualize nuclei at different stages of the cell cycle. * **Deep Learning Integration:** Implementation of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for robust 3D segmentation: * **PlantSeg:** Optimized for cell wall-based tissue segmentation. * **Cellpose:** Utilized for high-precision nuclear segmentation and tracking. * **Format Compatibility:** Automated handling and pre-processing of raw Nikon `.nd2` files. ### Technical Stack: * **Algorithms:** PlantSeg, Cellpose (Convolutional Neural Networks). * **Tools:** Python, Napari (for proofreading and manual correction), Fiji/ImageJ. * **Application:** High-throughput characterization of cell geometry and proliferation patterns in the root apical meristem.
